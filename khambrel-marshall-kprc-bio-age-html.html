Submerged himself into journalism to make a difference in the lives of people, Khambrel Marshall always strives for the truth considering both sides of all stories he covers and continues to excel in the broadcasting field with his expertise and proficiency.

In The Newsroom: Khambrel posing with Harris County Judge Ed Emmet while an interview on Houston Newsmakers, KPRC on November 29. 2018 (Photo: Khambrel's Facebook)

Khambrel Marshall is an American award-winning broadcast journalist and meteorologist based in Sugar Land, Texas working for KPRC Local 2 channel. He received the prestigious Emmy award, for a feature series titled "Guardians at Sea." 

Volunteering for charitable causes committed to improving the lives of children and women have been a high priority for him. Khambrel has been honored as the March of Dimes Texas Communications volunteer of the year in 2004 and 2006.

He has received the National Community Service Award and named as "Outstanding Young Floridian"  for the humanitarian efforts he made in the wake of Hurricane Andrew in 1992. He is one of the Board of Directors of the YMCA of Greater Houston, the March of Dimes, Texas Gulf Coast, and is a trustee for Goodwill Industries of Houston.

Let’s Know Khambrel’s Bio (Age)

Khambrel was born on in Monterey California in an army family. His date of birth is 17th December 1952 which makes his age 66 in 2018.

His father Cambrel B. Marshall served for 30 years in the United States Army receiving many honors including seven Bronze Stars and also gave back to the community volunteering in several organizations. Khambrel's father and mother are both no more with him. His mother died in 1983 out of cancer and his father died in 2017.

He has two wonderful sisters who accompany him on family occasions. Khambrel attended Seaside High School for his primary education and then got his undergraduate degree in Journalism - Print & Broadcast at Arizona State University. He also holds a Broadcast Meteorology Certification from Mississippi State University.

While most of his bio-details are open to the public eyes, he has yet to reveal his actual height.

Find Khambrel’s Career at KPRC & His Salary

Khambrel describes himself as a weather geek, and his first job in television was as a weekend sports anchor in Phoenix.

He served as the anchor, manager, and meteorologist at different Post news week television stations for 31 years starting from 1979 to 2010.

In 1985, he joined WPLG TV and served as a sports director for 6 years. Then he joined WFOR-TV as a Director/Anchor/Reporter and worked for 8 years.

He joined KPRC in 1999 and is working there from the past 18 years as a member of the KPRC Local 2 Severe Weather Team and host of the weekly topical news show, Houston Newsmakers. Khambrel gives the weekend evening weather forecasts to help people plan their day.

The average salary of a KPRC meteorologist in Houston is $66,690 per year but having an experience of 38 years in journalism, he must be making a handsome salary.
