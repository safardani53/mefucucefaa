Who are the NCT Dream members?

 Posted on April 6, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  786 words  |  Larita Shotwell

NCT DREAM is the third sub-unit of the South Korean boy band NCT.

The group was formed in 2016 by SM Entertainment with six members and they now have 23 with five sub-units.

Who are the NCT Dream members?

NCT Dream is made up of seven members.

Those members include:

Mark (Full name: Mark Lee)

Mark is a Canadian rapper, singer, dancer, and songwriter, who was born on August 2, 1999, in Toronto, Canada.

He first joined NCT in 2016 and has since become a member of its sub-units, NCT U, NCT 127, and NCT Dream.

He is also a member of SuperM.

Renjun (Full name: Huang Renjun)

Renjun is a Chinese singer who was born on March 23, 2000, in Jilin City, China.

He first joined NCT in 2016 and has since become a member of its sub-units NCT Dream and NCT U.

Since 2021, he has also been a member of SM Town.

Jeno (Full name: Lee Je-no)

Jeno is a South Korean rapper, singer, dancer, model, and television host who was born on April 23, 2000, in Incheon.

He began his career as a child commercials model and was later discovered by SM Entertainment at the age of 13.

In 2016, he then joined NCT and has since become a member of its sub-units NCT Dream and NCT U.

He is also a member of SM Rookies and SM Town.

Outside of NCT, he is known for his roles in the film Love Me Not (2006) and the TV series A-TEEN.

Haechan (Full name: Lee Dong-hyuck)

Haechan is a South Korean singer who was born on June 6, 2000, in Seoul.

He first joined NCT in 2016 and has since become a member of its sub-units NCT U, NCT 127, and NCT Dream.

Jaemin (Full name: Na Jae-min)

Jaemin is a South Korean dancer, rapper, and actor, who was born on August 13, 2000, in Jeonju-si.

He first joined NCT in 2016 and has since become a member of its sub-units NCT U and NCT Dream.

Outside of NCT, he is known for his roles in A-TEEN and Method to Hate You.

Chenle (Full name: Zhong Chenle)

Chenle is a Chinese singer and actor who was born on November 22, 2001, in Shanghai.

He began his career as a child performing at concerts and on TV shows in China and then, at the age of nine, he became the youngest singer to be invited to perform solo at the Golden Hall of Vienna.

In 2016, he then joined NCT and has since become a member of its sub-unit, NCT Dream.

He is also a member of SM Town.

Jisung (Full name: Park Ji-sung)

Jisung is a South Korean singer and dancer who was born on February 5, 2002, in Seoul.

He first joined NCT in 2016 and has since become a member of its sub-units, NCT Dream and NCT U.

In 2021, Jisung was also a member of SM Town.

What was the NCT graduation system?

When NCT started in 2016, it introduced K-pop fans to a rotational graduation system.

Basically, NCT's members would leave the group after turning 20 years old to make room for fresh, young talent, according to NME.

Mark was the first member to "graduate," having left the group in December 2018, however, he was brought back in 2020, when the graduation system was scrapped.

“The graduation system was traumatic but we had to endure it and go on,” Mark told Rolling Stone UK.

“Coming back in, to be honest, I don’t think I was fully relieved [about it],” he continued.

“I didn’t know how everyone, including the fans, were going to react and I didn’t know how I should react. I felt like it might complicate things too much, but it also meant we could do something as 7Dream that we’d always dreamed of.

"It felt like the destiny of NCT DREAM kinda, y’know, took a turn.”

While Mark might've had his reservations, the NCT fanbase welcomed him back with open arms and they have since been able to enjoy music from all of their favorite NCT members.

Who founded NCT?

Like most K-pop bands, NCT was formed with several members who already had successful careers in their own right.

The band is also made up of members from all around the world and was first formed by South Korean business executive and SM Entertainment founder Lee Soo-man.

Over the years, Soo-man has worked with several K-pop artists, including Super Junior, Girls’ Generation, SHINee, Red Velvet, SuperM, EXO, and Aespa, according to his Variety500 biography.

He also helped create the TV show K-Pop Goes Hollywood and messaging app Dear U Bubble.
