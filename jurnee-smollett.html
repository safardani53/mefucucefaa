Career

Regarding her career, Jurnee Smollett began her acting career in 1992 when she appeared on Martin and Out All Night. She then appeared as Denise Frazer in the ABC family comedy Full House and Hangin’ With Mr. Cooper. In 1996, she made her feature film debut in Francis Ford Coppola’s Jack.

Similarly, in the 2000 film Beautiful Joe, Jurnee co-starred with Sharon Stone and Billy Connolly. In the 2001 television film Ruby’s Bucket of Blood, she played Angela Bassett’s daughter. In 2006, she also appeared in the drama flick Gridiron Gang.

Furthermore, she portrayed Samantha Booke in the 2007 historical picture The Great Debaters, which was produced by Oprah Winfrey and Harvey Weinstein. The next year, she returned to television, appearing in two episodes of the ABC show Grey’s Anatomy.

Jurnee also appeared in the CBS legal drama The Defenders from 2010 to 2011, alongside Jim Belushi and Jerry O’Connell.

Jurnee also appeared as the lead in Tyler Perry’s 2013 drama film Temptation: Confessions of a Marriage Counselor. She was cast as the lead in the WGN America historical drama series Underground in 2015.

She also played Black Canari in the 2020 film Birds of Prey and Letitia Lewis in the HBO series Lovecraft Country. In November 2021, she will star alongside Tommy Lee Jones and Jamie Foxx in Maggie Betts’ courtroom drama The Burial, produced by Amazon Studios.

Jurnee Smollett Awards

Jurnee has received numerous honors throughout her career. In 1997, she won the Critics’ Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer. She received the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Youth Actress in 1999 and 2000.

She won the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress in a Motion Picture again in 2008. In 2021, she received the Critics’ Choice Super Award for Best Actress in a Horror Series.
