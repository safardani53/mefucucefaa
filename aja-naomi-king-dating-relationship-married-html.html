Aja Naomi received NAACP Image Award nomination of Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2015.

Aja Naomi Led Successful Acting Career From Early Age

Born on 11 January 1985, Aja Naomi King was raised in Walnut, California receiving a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Acting from the University of California. She attended Yale University’s School of Drama in 2010 and performed in several productions like Little Shop of Horrors, A Midsummer Night’s Dream, and Angels in America: A Gay Fantasia on National Themes.

With the support of her parents, Aja successfully made her television debut in the CBS police procedural, Blue Bloods in 2010 and eventually made her feature film debut in Damsels in Distress in 2011. It was in 2012 that Aja landed her big break as the series’ primary antagonist on the CW medical drama series Emily Owens, M.D. as Cassandra Kopelson.

The 33-year-old actress later had a recurring role in the ABC medical drama series, Black Box as Ali Henslee. She was cast as one of the lead characters in the ABC legal thriller How to Get Away with Murder in February 2014.

Her fame shot to the sky when she played the female lead role in the historical drama movie, The Birth of a Nation in 2016 that was based on the story of the 1831 slave rebellion led by Nat Turner. This led to Aja as a cast in The Upside in 2017 alongside Kevin Hart as a remake of the 2011 French film, The Intouchables.
