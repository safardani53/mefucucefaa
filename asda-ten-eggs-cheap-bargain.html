A BARGAIN lover has sparked a fierce debate after sharing a box of eggs that she had nabbed for a mere 50p.

The average price of a pack of six eggs is more than £2, so when shopper Jordan Alice Stretton, from Somerset, saw a bargain deal of ten eggs for just 50p she didn't think twice.

According to the young woman, you could nab a box of ''Quality Free Range eggs'' at Asda and one of this breakfast essential would cost you 5p.

Not one to gatekeep, Jordan also shared the cheap find on the popular Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK Group on Facebook - however, the post soon left social media users divided.

Many, like the bargain fanatic were impressed, but others noted that the low price came at a cost - and it was the farmer paying for it.

One member of the page said: ''Well done but I would be concerned about how much of that 50p the farmer would be receiving especially as their costs have gone up for energy,bird feed etc.''
