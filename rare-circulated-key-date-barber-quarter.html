A QUARTER minted in San Francisco in 1901 was recently sold on eBay for $3,348 after a fierce bidding war.

This antique coin is part of the Barber quarter series, named for Charles E. Barber, who designed 25-cent pieces from 1892 to 1916.

Barber quarters are known for the 13 stars on both front and back of the coin, as well as Lady Liberty facing right and wearing a laurel wreath on the heads, or obverse side.

Some Barber quarters are immensely valuable to collectors more than 100 years after their circulation ended, depending on their mint year and location.

For instance, the 1901-S quarter is a key date Barber coin, meaning it's rarer or harder to find than others in their series.

Who was Charles Barber?

According to the Smithsonian, Charles Barber was the sixth Chief Engraver of the US Mint.

He came to the United States from England with his family when he was twelve years old. 

His father William served as the fifth Chief Engraver for the Mint and passed the trade on to his son.

In 1869, Charles was brought on as a trainee.

Following William's death a decade later, Charles filled his father's role.

Value of 1901-S Barber quarters

There are three key date Barber quarters coveted by collectors, according to the Barber Coin Collector's Society (BCCS).

In addition to the 1901-S, Barber coins made in San Francisco in 1896 and 1913 are extremely valuable.

However, the 1901 is "the king of all regular issue Barber coinage," according to BCCS.

As with any coin, the value of a 1901-S Barber quarter depends on its condition, but it'll be worth a pretty penny regardless.

Circulated coins are graded from one to 60 based on their quality, and this recently sold quarter was professionally appraised by the Numismatic Guaranty Company with a grade of just three.

Despite the obvious wear and tear on this coin, it still garnered serious attention from collectors.

It was initially listed for just 99 cents, but 15 bidders exchanged 50 offers before the rare piece finally sold for just short of $3,350.

In great condition, 1901-S Barber quarters can sell for more than $40,000, such as a coin graded AU-55 that sold for $43,000 in 2021.

How to find rare coins

There are many ways you can find rare coins.

The first thing you’ll want to do is check places in your home or any places you might store change.

You can also try using a metal detector in known areas for buried treasure or stopping by your local bank and asking for a roll of coins.

To check if your coins are worth anything on eBay, search the full name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value.”

This way, you'll get a sense of what collectors are willing to pay for your coins.

You can either choose to sell the coin on eBay or through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.

And also - be aware of potential fakes when buying online.

To avoid this, it's worth checking the seller’s history and whether or not the coin was certified in the listing.
