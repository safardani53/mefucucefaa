When do the NFL playoffs start?

The 2024 NFL playoffs are scheduled to kick off Saturday, Jan. 13, 2024, and Sunday, Jan. 14, 2024, with the Super Wild Card Weekend games. The NFL on CBS will present the Steelers-Bills Wild Card game live on Sunday, Jan. 14, 2024, at 1 PM, ET, from Highmark Stadium in Orchard Park, New York.  

The chase for the Vince Lombardi Trophy concludes on Sunday, Feb. 11, 2024, with Super Bowl LVIII. 

In all, there are four rounds of the NFL playoffs: the Wild Card round; the Divisional round; the Championship round; and, finally, the Super Bowl. The 2024 NFL on CBS playoff schedule boasts games at each of these steps, including the 2024 Super Bowl. 

Who will play in the 2024 NFL playoffs?

The NFL playoffs boast the winners of the eight divisions (the AFC East, the AFC North, the AFC South, the AFC West, the NFC East, the NFC North, the NFC South, and the NFC West), plus six Wild Card teams – three from the AFC, and three from the NFC. In all, 14 teams make the NFL playoffs -- seven from the NFC and seven from the AFC. The seven AFC teams are whittled to two who square off against each other in the AFC Championship Game; the same applies to the NFC teams – they vie for spots in the NFC Championship Game. The winners of the Championship games advance to the Super Bowl.

Where will the playoff games be played?

In Wild Card, Divisional and Championship games, the higher-seeded team host its opponent. In the Super Wild Card Weekend Steelers-Bills game, for instance, the Steelers are the AFC's No. 7 seed, while the Bills are the AFC's No. 2 seed. Because they're the higher-seeded team, the Bills will host the playoff showdown at their Highmark Stadium in Orchard Park, New York.

The No. 1 seeds of the AFC and NFC each enjoy a bye during Super Wild Card Weekend. In the 2024 NFL playoffs, this means the Ravens and 49ers will advance directly to the Divisional round.
