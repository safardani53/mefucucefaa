Jeopardy! fans shocked as all 3 players miss 'handout' final question and already miss super-c

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  834 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

JEOPARDY! fans were shocked as all three players drew blanks on a "handout" final question on Thursday, and some accused their guesses weren't even written well.

Viewers missed super-champion Cris Pannullo who lost two nights ago.

One-day champ Ron Cheung, an economics professor from Ohio, faced A.Z. Madonna, a music journalist from Massachusetts (with a fitting name given her profession) and Sriram Krishnan, a consultant from Virginia.

Going into Final Jeopardy, Sriram had $21,800, Ron $10,800 and A.Z. $8,000.

The category was "Name's the same" and host Ken Jennings, 48 read: "A cocktail, an island & a WWII venture originally called 'Development of Substitute Materials' all bear this name."

All three contestants were incorrect and couldn't put together that the clue was looking for "Manhattan."

A.Z. wrote: "Mai Tai?", Ron "Martini" and shrugged, and Sriram also wrote "Martini" with what looked two "i's" that looked like "colons," fans said.

The newcomer bet $0 so he stood pat and won with $21,800.

Last night, fans were miffed that Cris' victor Andy Tirrell couldn't last more than one episode and in the second game without the power player things didn't go much better.

One upset fan wrote in a Reddit thread: "I can't be the only one shocked that nobody got the Final Jeopardy right. Seemed like a total gimme."

'NO-BRAINER'

Another agreed: "Really surprised no one got that Final Jeopardy correct with that many ways to come at the answer."

A third defended: "I think they got tripped up on the first two parts -drink and island make you think of something tropical. I got it by going straight to the third part."

A fourth wondered: "Whenever I think Final Jeopardy is a no-brainer, nobody gets it right. What’s up with that?"

As a fifth accused: "Did anyone else think that Sriran's i's looked more like colons in his response? I thought it said 'Ma: Ta:' when I first saw it."

"Seemed like a sloppy game overall," harshly wrote a sixth.

Contestant AZ tweeted: "I was born at Mt. Sinai Hospital and spent the first 18 years of my life within 45 minutes train ride of Penn Station. I have no excuse. I saw 'island,' 'cocktail' and WWII, I ended up in the Pacific Theater, and I couldn’t get out."

Others expressed they were feeling nostalgic for the super-champ days of two episodes ago as one tweeted: "Aww man. I miss Cris Pannullo already."

Yet another posted: "Is anyone else still in love with Cris Pannullo?"

One more wrote on Twitter: "I literally sobbed when Cris lost. I can’t watch for a while now. I already can’t wait for the Tournament of Champions and to see him on TV again!"

SUPER-STREAK OVER

Cris, 38, is a customer success operations manager and former poker ace from Ocean City, New Jersey, who started competing on September 30.

Cris shockingly lost on Monday by missing a crucial Daily Double and floundering on Final Jeopardy writing "Little Mermaid" under the category "Plays" when he needed it most.

He had $12,600 going into the final round, leading his closest competitor, San Diego professor Andy, by just $700.

Pannullo incorrectly guessed that the play was "The Little Mermaid." The mistake cost him $11,201.

Tirrell recognized the play as "The Tempest" by William Shakespeare. He ended the game with winnings of $15,399.

Pannullo reacted to his loss with a disappointed head shake. 

He can at least boast of having the fifth-highest winnings in the game show's history and its sixth-longest streak.

He won $748,000 total and millions of fans with his sportsmanlike approach to the game and his looks were an added bonus for many.

Seventeen of his 21 wins were "runaways," meaning by Final Jeopardy no one could catch him, making fans think he was going to keep winning.

He also went 48 for 49 on Daily Double clues before his last game - Cris even won by so much per game that execs called losing to him getting "Pannulo'd."

He revealed he took the game so seriously that he had the three-ingredient lunch every day and would sweetly incorporate his girlfriend Heather's birthday into his final wagers.

Those who have won more games than Cris in Jeopardy! history are Ken Jennings (74 games), Amy Schneider (40 games), Matt Amodio (38 games), James Holzhauer (32 games) and Mattea Roach (23 games).

He has won more money than Mattea, thus putting him fifth on the earnings leaderboard.

'DISAPPOINTMENT FOR SURE'

Cris said it was a "disappointment for sure" in a bonus video.

“Any one of the Daily Doubles that I bet big on like today, I could have gotten wrong. It could have been the end. It could have happened in Game 1; It could have happened in Game 3.”
