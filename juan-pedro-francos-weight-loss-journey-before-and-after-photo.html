Before and After Juan Pedro Franco Weight Loss

Juan Pedro is making waves again when news of the former world’s heaviest man’s weight decrease broke. The guy who held the Guinness World Record for the heaviest person in 1996 saw a remarkable transformation after losing an amazing 52 stone. The Mexican almost died after weighing 93 stone and 9 lbs, but he has already begun to drop weight. Manuel Uribe, a Mexican who weighed 597 kilos in 2007, previously held the record. But he died in May of 2016.

He initially came to public attention earlier that year, when photographs of him being taken from his home in Aguascalientes to a hospital in Guadalajara for life-saving treatment appeared. Juan’s weight had made him bed-bound by the time he achieved the world record at the age of 32, needing the aid of eight people, and he was forced to wear a diaper since he couldn’t use a toilet. Juan attained a weight of 10 stone when he was just six years old and started to have problems with it. A minor injury exacerbated his situation, and by the age of 17, he weighed more than 36 stone.

“My body just followed its path without any control whatsoever,” Juan told Guinness World Records. I attempted to diet every day, but nothing worked, and I gave up.”

Juan Pedro Franco’s Life-Changing Operation

Juan Pedro began a weight-loss program in 2016. He’s shed 329 kilograms since then, down from a stunning 595 kilos, or nearly the weight of a male polar bear. He is presently 266 kg. After witnessing Juan’s cry for help, an obesity expert in Guadalajara, Mexico, agreed to operate on him. Juan awoke for the first time to undergo the life-changing surgery. While in the hospital, he was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes, thyroid issues, hypertension, and fluids in his lungs. By 2017, he had lost 26 stone and 10 pounds, which qualified him for the game-changing gastric band treatment.

Juan also continued to lose weight following the operation by changing his diet and workout regimen. He had shed one-third of his body weight by 2019. Juan has lost an incredible 52 stone in the previous three years, and he now weighs 41 stone. Juan commented on the shift on Telemundo in Mexico, saying,

“It’s amazing just to be able to pick up your arms and get up every day, get up for a glass of water or use the restroom.”

It is amazing to be able to walk about more freely and independently.” Mr. Franco is no longer in bed since he can move and exercise more to maintain his height.
