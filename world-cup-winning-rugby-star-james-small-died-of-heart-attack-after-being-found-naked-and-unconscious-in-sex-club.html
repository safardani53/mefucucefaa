WORLD Cup-winning Springbok star James Small was found naked and unconscious in a sex club before being rushed to hospital where he died of a heart attack, it has been reported.

South African newspaper Rapport said a woman at the “gentleman’s club” which boasts ladies for hire on its website, drove the 50-year-old stark naked to an accident and emergency unit.

She is reported to have rushed inside the Life Bedford Gardens hospital asking for a portable hospital bed telling staff the rugby player was too heavy to be put in a wheelchair.

Rapport cited sources at the Johannesburg hospital as saying nurses covered him in blankets and took him inside but doctors later pronounced the winger dead.

The driver of the vehicle allegedly told nurses he was a client of the notorious sex club, The Harem, where he had collapsed.

As a result of the revelations, South African police have asked to see toxicology reports and have confirmed that a judicial inquest has now been opened into the cause of his death - believed to be a suspected heart attack.

Rapport said Small had collapsed at The Harem which according to its Facebook page is a “gentleman’s club that caters for every need” offering an “adult entertainment service”.

The sex club is about a kilometre from the hospital.

Police had reportedly tried to unlock Small’s cell phone without success but could see from an open message that £175 had been withdrawn earlier.

Small was known as the “bad boy” of South African rugby during his five-year career between 1992 and 1997 during which he lifted the World Cup in front of Nelson Mandela in 1995.

In 1993 he was dropped after being involved in a huge punch up at a nightclub but won his place back and by the time his career was over he had won 47 caps and scored 20 tries.

In 2009 he revealed in an interview how he had once tried to commit suicide after breaking up with model Christina Storm before admitting that he had hit her.

After cutting his wrists he said he had woken up in a padded cell in a psychiatric hospital.

Christina is the mother of their 14-year-old daughter Ruby and he has another daughter by another relationship both of whom are said to be devastated by the loss of their dad.

Harley Davidson riding Small also admitted to abusing alcohol and cocaine and after retiring he went into coaching rugby and also set up a successful restaurant in Cape Town.

He said in the interview: “There have been low points. Life is not the easiest thing”.

Friends also revealed after his death that former President Nelson Mandela had phoned him at his mothers home in one of his darkest moments and helped him turn his life around.

The former Springbok’s brother in law Gavin Houlston said he would not comment on the circumstances surrounding the legends death adding: “James’s legacy speaks for itself.
