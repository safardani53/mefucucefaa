One of the star players during Croatia’s journey to the World Cup 2018 final, Domagoj Vida, is a Croatian professional soccer player who currently plays as a center-back for AEK Athens.

Domagoj Vida was born on April 29, 1989, in Nasice, SR Croatia, but he grew up in Donji Miholjac. He spent his entire youth career in Croatia at Osijek youth academy.

Despite his long career, he only came into the limelight after his exceptional performance at the World Cup.

Aside from Luka Modric, Vida was the best player for Croatia en route to their second-place finish at the World Cup 2018. In fact, Domagoj was probably the best defender in the tournament.

He is included in the 26-man squad of the Croatian National Team for the Qatar World Cup 2022. After his performance in the last world cup, more eyes will be focused on speculating how he will outdo his previous defending records on the big stage.

Besides, Vida has endured it all in his glorious career, from being dropped due to altercations to being the best defender at the World Cup.

Here you will find out about Domagoj’s early life to his present days with AEK Athens and all that happened in between. So without further ado, let’s get started.

Domagoj Vida | Early Life And Family

Domagoj was born on 29 April 1989 in Nasice, which lies in the eastern part of Croatia. His mother, Zeljka Vida, was a typical housewife, while his father, Rudika Vida, was a professional footballer.

Therefore, young Vida loved playing football with his brother, Hrvoje Vida, whenever they were not busy with their studies. In fact, even during school, the Croatian would think of nothing else than football.

As a result, Domagoj joined the youth academy of his local club, NK Osi. After that, young Vida stayed with the club for a few years before Osijek came in and swooped over the young prodigy.

It is at Osijek where the Croatian eventually made his professional debut.

Domagoj Vida | Professional Career & Achievements

NK Osijek

Soon after joining Osijek, Domagoj made his professional debut in 2006 at the tender age of 17.

Subsequently, the Croatian made only 12 appearances in his rookie season as he needed time to develop and adapt. Nevertheless, Vida had impressed his manager enough to warrant a starting place the next season.

And that is precisely what happened as the talented center-back cemented his spot in the starting 11. To illustrate, Vida appeared in 22 games for the club that season, starting in almost all of those matches.

After that, Domagoj stayed with the club for two years before switching to Bundesliga giants Bayer Leverkusen.

At the time of his transfer, the Croatian international had appeared in 90 games for Osijek, in which he scored six times and assisted four times.

Bayer Leverkusen

It was 1 July 2010 when Domagoj signed for the Bundesliga club, Bayer Leverkusen, in a $2.49 million contract from Osijek. Subsequently, it seemed like the Croatian center-back had made it into the big league, but it was not meant to be.

As Vida managed to make nine appearances for the whole season. The Croatian national appeared only once in the Bundesliga, and that too as a substitute.

Having joined the club in the hopes of becoming a mega-star, Domagoj’s time with Leverkusen turned out to be a disaster instead.

Dinamo Aagreb

After just one season with the club, Vida decided to move to find another club that would allow him more playing time.

Domagoj’s wish to get more playing time came true when the biggest club in Croatia, Dinamo Zagreb, reportedly signed him for a contract worth $1.35 million.

After that, the Croatian center-back became a stalwart in the Dinamo backline playing in 47 games while leading the club to the league title.

However, the season was not without controversy, as during a Champions League tie against Lyon; tv footage showed Vida winking just after Lyon had scored their fifth goal.

As a result, there were widespread rumors that the match was fixed, and Vida was a prime suspect. But, UEFA did not take any action against the club or the player in light of no proper evidence.

The following season with Dinamo turned out to be possibly the worst season of his career. For instance, Domagoj was involved in many altercations throughout the season, limiting his appearances to just 28 games.

On 24 September 2012, Vida was thrown out of the bus for opening a beer en route to a cup match.

Also, the Croatian international had many disagreements with his coach that got him benched for several games.

As a result, Dinamo Zagreb decided to sell Domagoj. Subsequently, many clubs showed interest in the talented defender, but in the end, Ukrainian powerhouse Dynamo Kyiv secured Vida’s services.

Dynamo Kyiv

Dynamo reportedly paid a transfer fee of $5.18 million to Zagreb for the Croatian center-back. After that, Domagoj spent five years with the capital-based club, where he had some wonderful moments.

For instance, Vida scored the winning goal against Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk to clinch the 2014-15 Ukrainian Premier League title for the first time in six years.

The following season, Dynamo Kyiv won the league title again as Domagoj again proved to be one of the more important players in the title-winning squad.

After that, the Croatian international remained with Dynamo for another two years before embarking on a new journey.

By the time Vida finished his time with Dynamo Kyiv, he had played a total of 161 games in which he had scored 13 goals and provided eight assists.

Besides, the Croatian soccer player won two Ukrainian Premier Leagues with the club. All in all, Vida’s time with Dynamo was full of highs and some lows.

Besiktas JK

Nevertheless, the Croatian defender decided that a new environment was needed to take his game to the next level. Likewise, that is precisely what Domagoj did as he moved to Turkish giants Besiktas as a free transfer in the winter of 2018.

Nonetheless, it did not affect Vida one bit as he continued his impressive form. As a matter of fact, the Besiktas center-back’s form was so outstanding that the club decided to make Domagoj their highest-paid player.

Unfortunately, even though Vida was earning a massive salary at Besiktas, he could not win any trophies in his two seasons with the club.

For the Besiktas JK, he made a total of 165 appearances finding the back of the net 16 times while making 2 assists.

AEK Athens

On July 31, 2022, the defender was signed by AEK Athens as a free transfer. He is reported to bag around $20k per week.

He has made 11 appearances for the club, so far.

National Team

Vida’s international career started on 23 May 2010 against Wales in a 2-0 win. Subsequently, after performing exceptionally well for the national team, Domagoj was included in the UEFA Euro 2012 squad.

However, he was featured once in the whole competition. 

Domagoj’s first goal for the national team was in a friendly match against South Korea on 20 September 2013.

Likewise, Vida’s first appearance at the World Cup came in the 2014 World Cup. But, he was benched THROUGHOUT the tournament.

Nevertheless, the defender gained valuable experience that helped him in the future. After that, Vida upped his game as he vowed to make himself a starting player by Euro 2016.

And that was exactly what Domagoj did as he played in three matches at the UEFA Euro 2016. Sadly, Croatia got eliminated at the hands of the eventual winner, Portugal.

Nonetheless, the Croatian national team had showcased enough ability to be considered one of the dark horses for the 2018 FIFA World Cup.

Subsequently, in the 2018 FIFA World Cup, Croatia reached the final with Vida’s outstanding defending and Luca Modric’s brilliant performance. In fact, Domagaj was the best player for Croatia after the Ballon d’Or winner in 2018, Luka Modric.

To illustrate, he scored a header against the host nation, Russia, in the quarter-final to give his side a 2-1 lead in extra time. Also, Vida assisted Ivan Perisic’s goal against France, giving Croatia the lead.

Eventually, France won the World Cup by beating Croatia 4-2. Nevertheless, the Croatian national team had exceeded all expectations, as no one thought they would reach the World Cup final.

Also, Domagoj’s reputation skyrocketed after his extraordinary performances at the tournament.

The defender captained his national team for the first time on 15 October 2018. Furthermore, the Croatian defender marked the special occasion by scoring in the 2-1 win over Jordan.

At the time of this writing, Vida has made 100 appearances for the national team while scoring four goals.

After his top-notch performance at the last World Cup, he is expected to exhibit exceptional performance on the big stage. He will undoubtedly be in the starting 11 for the Croatian National Team Qatar World Cup.

This might be his last World Cup, so he might be willing to give his 100 percent or even more if needed. Every player representing their respective country wishes to take the cup to their home, and so does Domagoj.
