A MUM has told how she dressed her son up in a £12 Christmas jumper twice before noticing a huge mistake on the front.

Suzanne Adams, 38, picked up the festive knitwear from Peacocks for her boy Hadley before she spotted the glaring error.

The mum-of-three, from Renfrewshire, told the Scottish Sun that she was getting ready to take him to visit Santa when she clocked it.

The navy blue jumper, which is covered in pictures of Santa, snowflakes and presents, was meant to have the message 'HO! HO! HO!" written on the front.

But it somehow managed to be printed backwards, and instead reads: "!OH !OH !OH".

Suzanne, a hairdresser, said that she originally thought her son had put it on back to front when she noticed it.

She said: "Honestly I don't know, I've not got a clue how that happened.

"He had it on twice before and then I noticed it when he put it on for a third time to go have breakfast with Santa.

"When he came down the stairs I was like 'Hadley you've got your jumper on back to front'.

"Then we checked the label and realised it wasn't back to front at all.

"I remember thinking, 'I can't believe I paid £12 and it says 'OH, OH OH'."

Suzanne shared a clip of the hilarious jumper on TikTok where it quickly went viral.

She posted a photo of her son wearing the festive top with the caption: "When you buy your son a Christmas jumper, can anyone spot what's wrong with it?"

It quickly racked up more than 350,000 views with more than 1,600 likes, 250 comments and shares.

Speaking of the clip, Suzanne said: "That one went viral.

"It got a lot of reaction.

"We bought it from Peacock and they are quite a big company and I tagged them in it so I was a bit surprised when there was no reaction from them."

Users flocked to the comments of the video to share their hilarity at the backwards message.

Some suggested that the jumper was maybe on inside out or the phone was on "selfie mode" on the camera, but Suzanne, who goes by @suzylonglegs31 on the app, clarified that was the way the jumper came.
