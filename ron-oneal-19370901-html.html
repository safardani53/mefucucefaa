He was born in Utica, New York, and raised in Cleveland, Ohio, as the son of a jazz musician who later worked in a factory. Later, he attended Ohio State University and became a member of Karamu House, an African-American theatre company based in Cleveland.

In 1969, he made his Broadway debut in Ceremonies in Dark Old Men. He won an Obie Award for his portrayal in a 1970 production of No Place to Be Somebody a year later.

In 1980, he divorced his first wife, Carol Tillery Banks, and married Audrey Pool, his second wife, until his death.
