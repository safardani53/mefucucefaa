Did Frank Reich Get Arrested? Rumor Debunked and Career Explore

Following his recent departure as the head coach of the Carolina Panthers, rumors concerning Frank Reich’s arrest have been circulating. Reich was named head coach of the Panthers earlier this year. The club sacked him on November 27 as the squad continued to perform badly under his guidance throughout the current season. While supporters continue to mock him online, the sacked coach is trending on the Internet due to his alleged arrest. Is this true? Continue reading this article to find out!

Frank Michael Reich, commonly known as Frank Reich, is a professional football coach in the United States. He was most recently the head coach of the National Football League’s Carolina Panthers. He was the head coach of the Indianapolis Colts for around five years until joining the Panthers in January 2023. Frank has served as a coach for several clubs, including the Arizona Cardinals, San Diego Chargers, and Philadelphia Eagles. People consider him more successful as a professional player than as a coach, based on his total record

Debunked Frank Reich Arrest Rumor: Why Is a Fired Coach Trending?

On November 27, Carolina Panthers owner David Tepper announced the termination of Frank Reich. Reich’s career with the Panthers came to an end with a 1-10 record, one of the worst in NFL history. Following this recent occurrence, it is logical that those who are curious about what will happen next in his NFL career are looking for him on the internet. Reich is presently trending for an unexpected reason.

There have recently been various web stories concerning his arrest, but none of them claim to be factual. Similarly, users are uploading videos on TikTok and Twitter (now X) with the message “Frank Reich has been arrested.” However, there has been no confirmed confirmation of his arrest. Reich, in reality, does not have a criminal record and has never been imprisoned in his life.

Frank Reich’s Career

Frank Reich was a quarterback in the National Football League for numerous teams for over a decade. He started his NFL career after being drafted in the third round by the Buffalo Bills in 1985. Frank, who was born in Free Port, New York, participated in baseball, football, and basketball while in high school. After graduating from Cedar Crest High School, he went on to play NCAA football at the University of Maryland as a backup quarterback to future NFL standout Boomer Esiason.

Reich spent most of his playing career as a backup quarterback for NFL clubs. Despite spending almost a decade with the Bills, he didn’t play in a game until 1989, when starting quarterback Jim Kelly was sidelined due to a shoulder injury. After being released by the Bills in 1994, he played for three more teams: the Carolina Panthers, the New York Jets, and the Detroit Lions. Reich left the NFL following the 1998 season. Bill Polian, a well-known football administrator, referred to him as the best quarterback in NFL history in 2014.
