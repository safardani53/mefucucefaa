MICHAEL Strahan has promoted his new movie project on Instagram while remaining missing from his Good Morning America post.

The morning show host shared a look at BS High, the football-centric documentary his company is producing about Bishop Sickamore High School.

As he explained it, "It's about everything that went on with them that came to light after they played a game against IMG Academy. They were blown out. A lot of stuff happened."

"Come to find out, maybe it wasn't a real school. Hmm. you got to see the documentary to find out whether it's true or not."

Michael's new clip showed off a bit of what viewers can expect from the film.

He captioned his video: "Did he think he was doing right or did he know he was doing wrong? You be the judge!

"Watch #BSHigh TONIGHT at 9 PM on @hbo and @streamonmax! @smacentertainment @conschwartz."

Though Michael was active sharing his film on social media, however, he has remained absent from his GMA post.

It's likely he's still coming back from dropping off daughters Isabella and Sophia, 18, at their respective colleges.

He hasn't indicated when he'll be returning to the show just yet.

'SO IRRITATING!'

Meanwhile, GMA fans were left divided over a small detail in Michael Strahan's latest Instagram post.

The beloved broadcaster and NFL Hall of Famer, 51, gave an update on his personal life on Monday as he helped his youngest daughter move into college.

In the series of snaps posted by the former New York Giant, Strahan headed for Duke University with Sophia ahead of her move.

During his visit, the two toured the campus, enjoyed some local barbecue, and headed for Target to stock up on the essentials.

"Dropped off the youngest of the tribe @sophialstrahan at college!" Strahan wrote.

"Can't believe how time has flown by and I’m so proud of her. I know she’s going to crush college life!! #DukeDad"

However, while most of the comments that followed consisted simply of well-wishers, fans, and fellow parents having also endured the typical college move-in Target run, some were left questioning why Strahan had been allowed to bring his dog Enzo into the store.

One comment read: "Pretty young lady, have a successful college year!

"On another note, I hate when people bring their dog in a store or restaurant!

"If it's not a service dog, then they ... should be not allowed!! Like wtf, leave your pets at home!! So irritating!!"

A second asked: "Why is the dog in a store?"

As a further fan joked: "Most dogs are better behaved than people."

Target technically does not allow dogs in their stores, with service dogs being the exception, as per Dogoday.

Strahan regularly posts sweet pics of his four-legged friend on Instagram.

Most recently, he took Enzo with him for lunch, although it seems the pup was less excited than Strahan.

"Lunch with Enzo! He is really talkative. LOLOL," Strahan captioned the pics which showed him beaming alongside a sleeping Enzo.

HEADING WEST

On Wednesday, Michael subtly revealed his whereabouts in a new post as he remains absent from Good Morning America.

He recently dropped his daughter, Sophia, off to college.

Michael, 51, has been absent from GMA, which films in New York City, this whole week.

Linsey Davis has been filling in for him this week.

Michael explained to his followers where he is and why he is not currently on the show with a subtle picture.

On Tuesday, he posted a photo to his Instagram story in which he was driving near Fairfax Avenue in Los Angeles when he saw a billboard for the show, BS High.

Just a few hours later, his daughter, Isabella, 18, posted a photo to her story.

"Last night," she captioned the post.

She was at a Drake concert in California, and the person he brought on stage wore a Los Angeles Lakers jersey.

Isabella is starting her freshmen year at the University of Southern California.

According to the USC calendar, classes began August 21.

TIME FLIES!

Just a few days prior, Michael traveled to Durham, North Carolina, to drop off his other daughter, Sophia, 18, to college.

She is a freshmen at Duke University.

Michael captioned his photos: "Dropped off the youngest of the tribe @sophialstrahan at college! Can’t believe how time has flown by and I’m so proud of her. I know she’s going to crush college life!! #DukeDad"

He also added: "I know she’s a twin so @isabellastrahan pics coming soon! Lol"

He grinned alongside Sophia as the pair posed in front of the school, a restaurant, and in Target.

They spent some time shopping for the essentials Sophia would need to get started at school.

Although orientation started over the weekend for Duke students, they do not begin classes until the 28th.

MAKING MOVES

Michael has been absent from the morning show, but he's been promoting his other business ventures.

While still away from the show, the 51-year-old shared a photo of himself in a button-down shirt to his Instagram Stories.

He captioned the photo: "Raise your shirt game with our latest styles @mooresclothing."

Moores is a retailer that sells some of Michael Strahan's clothing line.

In the photo, Michael wore one of his Modern Fit 4-Way Stretch shirts.

He added the hashtag "#RaiseYourGame" to the post featuring himself posing in the shirt.
