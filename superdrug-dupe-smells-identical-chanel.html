SHOPPERS are rushing to buy a £6 Superdrug perfume which smells "identical" to a £126 Chanel fragrance.

Superdrug's latest budget "dupe", the 50ml Artiscent Atelier Eau de Parfum Lily Amour, is said to smell exactly like Chanel Chance, saving shoppers £120.

  • Artiscent Atelier Eau De Parfum Lily Amour 50ml, £11.99, Superdrug - buy here

The dupe is currently discounted in Superdrug's sale, with a buy one get one half price deal also on offer.

It's priced at £11.99, but this offer means you'll be able to get your hands on it from £6.

Superdrug told the Daily Record that its Artiscent range is "created in Paris, home of prestige perfumery, where perfumers are given carte blanched to translate their vision into unique and exciting scents".

It described its Lily Amour dupe as smelling of transparent jasmine, water lily, mandarin zest and musky vanilla.

The chain's website has been flooded with five-star reviews of the dupe, with many customers comparing its scent to Chanel Chance.

One shopper said: "One day a couple of years ago, I met a woman on a train who was wearing a lovely perfume.

"It smelled like warm, rich honey on a summer's day.

"She told me it was called Chance.

"Then I heard about this one. YES! Almost identical and a tenth of the price, too. I'm hooked.

"It's certainly good enough - and cheap enough - to wear every day. And I do."

Other shoppers praised the dupe as a long-lasting and affordable work perfume.

The Artiscent Atelier Eau de Parfum Lily Amour 50ml perfume has had its price slashed from £11.99 to £5.99 in Superdrug's half-price sale, and can be paired with another Artiscent fragrance in the chain's BOGOF deal.

It comes after two Superdrug workers revealed that the Artiscent range also includes dupes for Chloe Fleur de Parfum, Lancome La Vie Est Belle, and Dior Forever and Ever.

Home Bargains superfan Georgia says that the discount chain stocks dupes for Armani Si, Marc Jacobs Daisy, Jo Malone Pomegranate Noir and Chanel No.5.

Perfume fanatic Nicola Dolan discovered an entire shelf of Tom Ford dupes in Aldi.

She claimed the supermarket's £5.99 Sandalwood and Jasmine scent is a dupe for Tom Ford's Private Blend Santal Blush, which costs £490.
