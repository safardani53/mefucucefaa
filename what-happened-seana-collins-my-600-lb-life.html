What happened to Seana Collins on My 600-Lb Life?

 Posted on July 19, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  426 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

MY 600-Lb life is a reality TV series that chronicles the daily life and struggles of heavily overweight people.

The show aims to give the participants a better quality of life through gastric surgery and guiding them to obtain help to deal with food addictions.

What happened to Seana Collins on My 600-Lb Life?

Seana Collins’ life was introduced to My 600-Lb Life in early 2020 where she was struggling with her weight and body image. 

The 23-year-old lived with her mother in Kansas City, Missouri, and applied to be on the show as she was weighing nearly 700-Lbs.

She met with Dr. Younan Nowzaradan for the first time to go over her health and goals. 

She explained her expectations from the visit in the episode, saying: “I’m hoping if I can lose like Dr. Now tells me to lose and I can move down to Texas, that he’ll tell me I can do his program.”

Unfortunately, Seana only managed to lose one pound out of the 80 that was part of her goal, as instructed by Dr. Nowzaradan.

Where is Seana Collins now?

Seana reportedly was never able to qualify for weight loss surgery, according to Reality Blurb.

She managed to lose 30-Lbs but it was still not enough for the procedure.

Seana keeps a relatively low profile on social media and has not updated her fans on her weight loss journey since being on the show. 

Dr. Nowzaradan told Seana that he was willing to work with her if she lost 50-Lbs more and sought help for her previous mental health and addiction issues.

While working with her, Dr. Nowzaradan told Seana: “I know it's very hard when you are alone, but you need to decide if you want to change your situation and live, or if you want to continue on this path you are on.”

Weight loss surgery is a risk for the participants as complications may arise due to their excessive weight.

Former participant Coliesa McMillian passed away in September of 2020 shortly after the surgery due to severe complications and spending time in the ICU. 

Participant Annjeanette Whaley had alleged that producers had placed her on “an extreme diet” in order to qualify for the surgery. 

She was seeking $1million in damages in her lawsuit against the show.
